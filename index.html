1. What is an Adverb?

An adverb is a word that modifies, or describes, a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.

The Rule of Modification:

  • Modifies Verb: Jennifer smiled broadly.
  • Modifies Adjective: A very worthy contestant.
  • Modifies Adverb: Conversation ended rather abruptly.
Created by: Amanullah Khan

2. How, When, or Where?

When modifying a verb, an adverb typically describes how, when, or where the action is done.

How?

Erin approached cautiously.

When?

Try to get to school early.

Where?

Ron and I looked there.

Created by: Amanullah Khan

3. Adverb Formation

The -ly Pattern

Many adverbs are formed by adding -ly to adjectives.

Quick → Quickly

Soft → Softly

The -ly Trap

Not all words ending in -ly are adverbs!

The words friendly, lively, kindly, and lonely are usually adjectives.

Created by: Amanullah Khan

4. Adverbs without -ly

Many common adverbs do not end in -ly. You use these every day:

afterward • sometimes • later • often • soon • here • there • everywhere • not • fast • hard • long • straight

"We ran fast, but we were not soon enough."
